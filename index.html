Multi-layer transparent images with different styles generated by our data engine


Abstract

The generation of multi-layer transparent images with alpha channels has grown increasingly important in recent times. However, current approaches often experience aesthetic degradation when fine-tuned on low-quality crawled datasets. In this paper, we present a novel training-free pipeline that leverages state-of-the-art diffusion models to generate layered transparent images. This approach bypasses dataset-specific training and maintains visual fidelity. It generates high-quality transparent layers, each with accurate alpha mattes, thereby ensuring superior aesthetics for every layer.

Our key contributions include: (i) a generate-then-matting scheme for single-layer transparent image generation that combines diffusion transformers with a matting model. This scheme uses carefully selected suffix background prompts to establish precise attention patterns between visual tokens and suffix text tokens; (ii) a multi-layer synthesis engine that generates coherent semantic layouts and composites individual transparent layers into complete designs; and (iii) a Transparent Image Preference Scoring (TIPS) model for assessing layer quality.

Experimental results demonstrate that our framework generates visually appealing transparent images, surpassing existing state-of-the-art methods. Moreover, training the latest ART model with our high-quality synthesized data significantly improves its layer-wise aesthetic quality and overall visual appeal. This improvement reaches an unprecedented level, outperforming the original ART model by a large margin.



Method Overview


MultiLayerFLUX Pipeline

We first apply a multi-layer layout planner to generate a semantic layout from the given global prompt. Then, we apply LayerFLUX to generate high-quality transparent layers separately. Next, we use our trained Transparent Image Preference Score (TIPS) model to assess the quality of each transparent layer, and we abandon or regenerate any low-quality layers. Last, we further apply an artifact classifier to identify and filter out multi-layer images with severe artifacts.

We introduce LayerFLUX, a training-free pipeline that uses diffusion models to generate images on a uniform background and then applies state-of-the-art matting to extract precise alpha masks, delivering high-quality single transparent layers without any fine-tuning.
